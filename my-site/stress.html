Stress and Anxiety Management Tips

Simple, practical tools female athletes can use before, during, and after competition.

Home Articles Resources Contact
Tip 1

Control Your Breathing Before Competition

Pre-game nerves are normal, but they don't have to run the show. Slow, deliberate breathing calms the nervous system fast.

  • Inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 4, exhale for 6
  • Repeat for 1–2 minutes before you step onto the court or field
  • Use it again between points or plays if nerves spike
Tip 2

Separate Performance from Self-Worth

A bad match or a missed shot is information about that moment, not a verdict on your value as an athlete or a person.

  • After a mistake, name what happened factually, without judgment
  • Ask "what's the next useful action?" instead of "what does this say about me?"
  • Keep a short list of things you're proud of outside sport, and revisit it often
Tip 3

Build a Wind-Down Routine

Anxiety often spikes when the body stays "switched on" long after training or competition ends.

  • Set a consistent post-training routine: stretch, shower, eat, then rest
  • Limit scrolling social media in the hour after a game, win or lose
  • Write down one thing that went well, even on hard days
Tip 4

Know When to Ask for Support

Ongoing anxiety that doesn't ease with rest, breathing, or time off deserves more than self-management.

  • Talk to a coach, teammate, or family member you trust
  • Consider speaking with a sports psychologist or counselor
  • Treat asking for help as a training decision, not a personal failure
