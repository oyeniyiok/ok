Self-Care and Mindfulness Practices

Small, repeatable habits that help female athletes stay grounded on and off the field.

Practice 1

Start the Day Without Your Phone

The first few minutes after waking set the tone for how your nervous system runs the rest of the day.

  • Wait at least 15 minutes after waking before checking messages or social media
  • Use that time to stretch, hydrate, or simply sit in silence
  • Notice how differently the morning feels compared to reaching for your phone first
Practice 2

Body Scan Before Sleep

Athletes carry physical tension that's easy to ignore during the day but shows up at night as restlessness.

  • Lie down and slowly bring attention from your feet up to your head
  • Notice tight spots without trying to fix them, just observe
  • Pair with slow breathing for 5 minutes before sleep
Practice 3

Journal After Training, Not Just After Games

Reflection shouldn't only happen after big wins or losses. Regular, low-stakes journaling builds self-awareness over time.

  • Write 2–3 sentences after each training session: how you felt, not just what you did
  • Track patterns over weeks rather than judging any single entry
  • Keep it private and honest, this is for you, not a coach or audience
Practice 4

Protect One Non-Sport Activity

Identity that's built entirely around performance is fragile. A hobby, friendship, or interest outside sport gives you somewhere to stand when results wobble.

  • Choose one activity each week that has nothing to do with competing or training
  • Treat it as non-negotiable, the same way you would a training session
  • Let it be something you do for enjoyment alone, not for progress or metrics
