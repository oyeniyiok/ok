Mental Health

Short reads on the parts of an athlete's life that never show up on the scoreboard.

01 Foundations

Why Mental Health Matters in Women's Sports

Mental health is just as important as physical health. Female athletes often carry pressure from competition, academics, family expectations, and social media — sometimes all at once, and often without anyone acknowledging how much they're holding.

Recognizing these challenges is the first step toward building resilience. Naming the pressure doesn't make an athlete weaker; it's usually what makes the difference between a career that lasts and one that quietly burns out.

02 Awareness

Recognizing Burnout

Burnout can look like constant fatigue, a loss of motivation, dips in performance, and emotional exhaustion that doesn't lift after a good night's sleep.

It rarely announces itself all at once — it builds quietly. Early support, from a coach, a teammate, or a trusted person off the court, can prevent a short rough patch from turning into a long-term issue.
